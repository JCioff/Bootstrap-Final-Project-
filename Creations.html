Madison - The Offical Magazine of JMU

Content

Above are some covers I created for the Madison Magazine cover contest taking place now throughout November 2020 leading up to the election. These were done as a project for another class taken during the Spring of 2020. The idea behind the contest was to create a compelling cover for the Fall 2020 edition of the magazine's publication, however the incentive behind creating them was quite a challenge. The platform we operated around necessitated a non-partisan, apolitical call to strengthen democracy. My ideas were farily limited, yet I was able to create a few covers which sprung this idea to life. Both the left and center covers are comprised of vector images I created with Adobe Illustrator, and the cover to the right is a raster image taken by me this past summer at the White House in Washington D.C.

Quentin Tarantino

For as long as I can remember, I have always loved and hailed Quentin Tarantino as my all-time favorite director. From his very first works to his very last, I have seen each and every one of his films multiple times. This picture was inspired by another photo I saw having the "Supreme" sticker slapped over one of his movie posters. Loving both the over-popular hybebeast street culture as well as the cinematic works of the John Wayne-Martin Scorcese-like director, this was a really fun piece to put together.
